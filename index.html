Awareness & Management

Reduce

The first and most important step. Plan your meals, make shopping lists, and store food properly to prevent spoilage and overbuying.

Reuse

Turn leftovers into new meals or use food scraps to create vegetable broth. Get creative and find new purposes for excess food.

Recycle

For what can't be eaten, composting is key. Learn how to turn food waste into nutrient-rich soil for your garden.

Why This Matters

Environmental Impact

When food is thrown away, it often ends up in landfills. As it decomposes, it releases methane, a greenhouse gas that is far more potent than carbon dioxide. Reducing food waste is a powerful way to combat climate change.

A Valuable Resource

Food waste isn't garbageâ€”it's a valuable resource. Through processes like anaerobic digestion, it can be converted into biogas to generate clean energy and a nutrient-rich digestate that acts as a powerful fertilizer, enriching our soil.

More Ways to Help

Shop Smarter, Waste Less

Creating a meal plan and a detailed shopping list before you go to the store is a powerful way to reduce waste. It helps you buy only what you need, saving money and preventing food from going bad.

Store Food the Right Way

Proper storage can extend the life of your food significantly. Learn which items belong in the fridge, pantry, or freezer to keep them fresh for longer and avoid unnecessary spoilage.

Make a Difference at Home

Composting is an easy and effective way to manage food waste. By turning scraps into nutrient-rich soil, you help the environment and can even create a healthy garden.

Donate Excess Food

If you have surplus food that is still good, consider donating it to local food banks, shelters, or charities. This helps those in need and prevents food from being wasted.

Know Your Dates

Many people confuse "best before" and "use by" dates. "Best before" refers to quality, not safety. Learning the difference can save you from throwing away perfectly edible food.

Join the Community Effort

Look for local food sharing apps, community gardens, or food rescue programs. Getting involved with your community can amplify your impact and help create a more sustainable food system.
